Hello, I am Carlton Smith! I am a senior undergraduate physics and math student at the University of Florida. For a bit of background, I entered UF with an interest in planetary science and astronomy, allured by the opportunity to contribute to our mapping of space, 'the final frontier'. Steadily, as time has progressed I've grown interested in the physics of complex systems, especially those found in living matter. In the later portion of my undergraduate, I've had the privilege of working in research experiences related to deep learning, image denoising, machine learning, and neuroscience. To be more specific, my current research involves algorithmically visualizing and measuring the similarity between spatial components of neural activity across sessions and specimens. My research interests include theoretical and computational biophysics and neuroscience - specifically, applications of statsitical physics to studying complex biological systems.

In my free time you can catch me cooking, listening to music, practicing my bass or guitar, reading, or exploring cityscapes!
